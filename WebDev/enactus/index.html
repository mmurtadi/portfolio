WE ARE WINDSOR!

We are a local chapter of Enactus, a non-profit organization that seeks to better the lives of others using the power of entrepreneurship.

We are a community of student, academic, and business leaders that strive to empower local members, instil entrepreneurial action, and enhance the economic health of our community by enabling the progress of our student leaders and establishing sustainable projects throughout the Windsor-Essex region.

Project Releaf!

Project Releaf focuses on creating solutions to a community’s problem through the creation of local entrepreneurs with environmental sustainable business ideas.

Project Releaf has an extensive background with developing entrepreneurs in South Africa and Morocco who have established and sustained businesses that give clean drinking water to the villagers through the creation and distribution of eco-friendly water filters.


Our current vision is to expand Project Releaf into Canada and concentrate on creating solutions to environmental issues.

CookStart!

CookStart is a one-on-one consulting program that aims to support individuals to develop a sustainable food business. These are individuals that have struggled to secure other employment alternatives due to a lack of education or particular skill sets and have a passion for food.

We recruit prospective clients through the New Canadian Centre of Excellence and Windsor-Women-Working-With-Immigrant-Women, among other community partners. Clients work with student consultants, financial planners, and restaurant industry leaders throughout the twelve week program.


CookStart empowers clients to generate a reliable stream of income, allowing them to improve the lives of not only themselves, but also their families.

Windsor SOUP!

Windsor SOUP is a monthly micro-funding dinner that brings the community together through a unique collaboration of social and environmental projects. Each project directly impacts the community and influences positive change. Presenters will have the opportunity to raise awareness while addressing the need and impact of their project.


Each person that attends makes a minimum donation of $5. This provides them with soup, salad, dessert, and a vote. Attendees will get the opportunity to socialize and learn about the many local projects that work to improve our community. Each event follows a 4:4:4 format; 4 projects present for 4 minutes, followed by 4 questions. At the end of the presentations votes are cast. The project that gets the most votes by the end of the night receives the collected donations at the door.


The P. and L. Odette Foundation also matches the amount raised throughout the night and donates it to the winner. In addition, Libro Credit Union donates $500 to the winning project. Each project gets the opportunity to network with community members and receive valuable feedback and ideas for their venture. This allows the projects to grow and evolve while raising awareness of local initiatives. 

CityThrive!

CityThrive is a 12 week program that provides business consultation to entrepreneurs on social assistance. Through our curriculum, we provide instruction on business fundamentals such as marketing, operations, financing and market analysis.


Consultants in this program will be partnered up with a client and will help them work through the curriculum and develop a business plan, as well as forge a relationship that has the potential to last a lifetime. 

Youthrive!

Youthrive is a 10 week program empowering gradeschool, highschool and at-risk youth through entrepreneurship. In 10 weeks, students effectively run their own microbusiness with the help of one-on-one mentorship, curriculum based learning, and a micro loan from the WFCU.


After the program, top ventures from each school compete at Youthx in front of a panel of judges, with the top 3 groups receiving investments from various sponsors. Youthrive has expanded to over 30 schools this year, representing substantial growth and potential for future entrepreneurs in Windsor/Essex.
